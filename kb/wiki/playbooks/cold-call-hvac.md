# Cold Call Playbook — HVAC / Plumbing / Electrical

Dial-ready decision tree. Read top to bottom. Indent = their response. **Bold** = your line.

> **Thesis anchor:** this playbook operationalizes [[positioning/after-hours-wedge]]. Every branch funnels toward the after-hours gap before pitching. If you find yourself pitching in §1, stop and go to §2.

---

## 0. OPENER

**"Hey [Name], Rashid here — cold call, you can hang up in 20 seconds if you want. Quick one: most HVAC shops I talk to are solid 8 to 5, but evenings and weekends get messy. How are you handling after-hours calls right now?"**

*The 20-second permission line earns you the next 90. The framing does the work — you've already skipped past "we answer it."*

---

## 1. AFTER-HOURS PIVOT

Whatever they say, your goal is to get them describing their after-hours setup in their own words.

### → "Goes to voicemail" / "They leave a message"
**"Yeah, that's where it gets expensive. Rough guess — how many of those voicemails on a typical Saturday?"**
→ Go to §2.

### → "We have an answering service"
**"Got it — are they actually booking jobs live, or mostly taking a message for you to call back Monday? Because the ones I've seen, by the time you call back the customer's already booked with someone else."**
→ If "taking messages" → §2. If "they book live" → §3.

### → "My wife / dispatcher / CSR handles it"
**"Makes sense. Is she actually on the phone at 9pm on a Saturday, or is it more like she checks voicemails in the morning?"**
→ Almost always the second. Go to §2.

### → "We don't miss calls" / "We're covered"
**"That's impressive — are you picking up live at 9pm on a Saturday, or is it going somewhere? Just curious how you've got it set up."**
→ Forces them to describe the system. 80% of the time they describe a gap. Go to §2.

### → "We already tried AI" / "We tried [competitor]"
**"Totally fair. Was the problem that it sounded robotic, or that it couldn't actually qualify and book the job right?"**
→ Either answer is your opening. Go to §3.

### → "Depends" / "It varies" / "Sometimes"
**"Yeah, that's usually what I hear. Where do the misses mostly happen — nights, weekends, or when you're already slammed on a job?"**
→ Multiple choice = easy. Whatever they pick, go to §2.

### → "What is this?" / "What are you selling?"
**"We help HVAC shops answer, qualify, and book inbound calls live when you can't pick up — especially after hours. I was just trying to see how you're handling that today. Mind if I ask?"**
→ Loop back to §1.

### → "Not interested" / "I'm busy"
**"Totally fair — one question and I'll let you go: when a new customer calls you Saturday night, what actually happens to that call?"**
→ One more shot. If they answer, you're back in §1. If they hang up, they hang up.

### → "I'm on a job right now"
**"I figured — that's actually why I called. When you're tied up like this and a new customer calls, where does it go?"**
→ The fact that they answered YOU while on a job proves the gap. Go to §2.

---

## 2. QUANTIFIER (never skip this)

**"Rough guess — how many after-hours calls do you think come in on a typical weekend? Even just Saturday."**

→ They give a number (any number).

**"And what's one of those jobs worth on average — ballpark?"**

→ They give a number.

*(Do the math in your head. Don't say it out loud yet. Let it sit.)*

**"Okay — so even if half of those turn into real jobs, that's [$X] a weekend sitting in a voicemail box. Does that sound about right to you?"**

→ If they agree (or don't disagree): go to §4.
→ If they push back ("nah, less than that"): **"Fair — what's your gut on the real number?"** Then go to §4.

---

## 3. SERVICE-SPECIFIC WEDGES

### They use an answering service that "books live"
**"Nice — what's the booking rate look like? Because the industry average is around 20% and most owners don't realize it until they see it."**
*Not attacking — introducing doubt with a number.*

### They tried AI that sounded robotic
**"Yeah, most of the 2023 stuff was rough. Have you heard a recent one? I can text you a 30-second clip of one of ours handling a real after-hours call — no pitch, just so you hear the difference."**
*Low-friction yes. Ask for phone number. Now you have a lead even if the call ends.*

### They tried AI that couldn't book
**"That's the exact thing we fixed — ours actually asks the qualifying questions your CSR would ask and drops the booking into your calendar. Worth 15 minutes to show you?"**

---

## 4. PITCH (only after §2)

**"Got it. Here's what we do, short version: when a customer calls after hours and you can't pick up, our system answers live, sounds like a person, asks the same questions your best CSR would, and drops the booked job straight into your calendar. You wake up Monday with jobs instead of voicemails."**

*(Pause. Let them react.)*

**"Worth 15 minutes this week to see if it'd fit your setup? I can show you a real call from a shop like yours."**

---

## 5. CLOSE

### → "Yeah, sure"
**"Awesome. I've got Thursday at 10 or Friday at 2 — which works?"**
*Two options. Never "when works for you."*

### → "Send me info first"
**"Happy to — what's the best email? And just so I send the right thing, are you more worried about after-hours, overflow during the day, or both?"**
*You got the email AND a qualifier. Follow up in 48 hours with a one-line: "Here's that 30-second call clip — [link]. Thursday 10 or Friday 2 to walk through it?"*

### → "Call me back next week"
**"Will do — Tuesday or Wednesday better?"**
*Pin it. "Next week" without a day = dead lead.*

### → "Not right now"
**"No problem. Can I send you one 30-second clip of a real after-hours call so you've got it when the timing's right?"**
*Consolation lead. You're building a list.*

---

## 6. IRON RULES

1. **Never pitch before §2.** The number is the whole sale.
2. **Never say "AI" first.** Say "system" or "we answer the call." Let them ask.
3. **Never defend the price.** They haven't asked. If they do: "Depends on volume — usually pays for itself in 2-3 booked jobs a month. Want me to run your numbers on the demo?"
4. **Always get a phone or email before hanging up.** Even a "no" lead is worth a clip.
5. **The call is won or lost in §1.** If you can't get them to describe their after-hours setup, nothing downstream matters.

---

## 7. ONE-PAGE CHEAT SHEET

Print this. Tape it next to the phone.

```
OPENER → "8-to-5 solid, but evenings get messy — how are you handling after-hours?"

THEIR ANSWER → Pivot line (§1)

QUANTIFY → "How many Saturday calls?" + "What's a job worth?"

MATH → "That's $X sitting in voicemail. Sound right?"

PITCH → "Answers live, books the job, calendar Monday morning."

CLOSE → "Thursday 10 or Friday 2?"

FALLBACK → "Can I send you a 30-sec clip?"
```
