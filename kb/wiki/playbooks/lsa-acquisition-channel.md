# Google LSA: Where CallLock's Customers Are Burning Money

Yes — they are spending money on Google LSA ads. And they're wasting a huge chunk of it on missed calls. This is CallLock's acquisition channel.

## The Numbers

**~70% of contractors in most markets run LSA ads** (up from 28% in 2021). This is mainstream, not early-adopter.

**They're spending $300-$1,200/week** on LSA ads. That's $1,200-$5,000/month just on Google lead gen.

**Cost per lead by trade:**
| Trade | Cost per lead |
|-------|--------------|
| HVAC | $45-$110 |
| Plumbing | $35-$85 |
| Electrical | $30-$70 |
| Roofing | $60-$130 |

**At a 31% booking rate and $50 avg CPL, a booked job costs ~$161 in ad spend.**

## The Missed Call Problem Is Built Into LSA

Here's why this matters for CallLock:

**Google punishes missed calls.** If your answer rate drops below 80%, your LSA ranking suffers. Miss more than 5% of calls, and Google starts showing competitors instead. Every missed call literally costs you future leads, not just the current one.

**Response speed determines who wins.** 35-50% of sales go to the first responder. Respond within 5 minutes and you're 8x more likely to convert vs. waiting 30 minutes.

**45% of LSA leads are unbookable** (spam, wrong numbers, out-of-area). This means contractors are paying for leads they can't use, AND missing real leads because they're drowning in junk calls.

**The math that sells CallLock:**
- Contractor spends $2,000/mo on LSA ads
- Misses 20-40% of the calls those ads generate (industry average)
- Each missed call = $50-$110 wasted in ad spend + lost revenue
- Missing 10 calls/month = $500-$1,100 in wasted ad spend alone
- CallLock at $297/mo pays for itself by answering 3-6 missed calls

## Why LSA Contractors Are CallLock's Best-Fit Customer

1. **They're already spending money to get phone calls.** They've crossed the "willing to pay for leads" threshold. $297/mo for call answering is marginal vs. their existing $1,200-5,000/mo ad spend.

2. **They have an acute, measurable problem.** Google literally tells them their answer rate is low. They can see the missed calls in their LSA dashboard. This isn't a theoretical pain — it's a metric they're staring at.

3. **They're incentivized to answer faster.** Google rewards responsiveness with better rankings. CallLock doesn't just save missed calls — it improves their LSA ranking, which means more leads for the same spend.

4. **They're NOT on ServiceTitan.** The LSA-heavy contractor is typically a small-to-mid shop. ServiceTitan targets $1M+ businesses with 20+ techs at $245-$398/tech/month. The LSA contractor is on Housecall Pro ($79-$329/mo), Jobber, or paper. This is exactly CallLock's target.

5. **The pitch writes itself.** "You're spending $2,000 a month on Google ads that ring your phone. How many of those calls go to voicemail? CallLock answers every one."

## How to Find Them

### Method 1: Google LSA Directory (free, immediate)
Search Google for "HVAC near me" or "plumber near me" in any market. The LSA results at the top ARE your prospects. You can see:
- Their business name
- Their phone number
- Their review count and rating
- Whether they're "Google Guaranteed"

A contractor with <50 reviews and a 4.0-4.5 star rating is likely a smaller shop — perfect fit.

### Method 2: LSA Responsiveness Signals
Call them. If it goes to voicemail during business hours, they need CallLock. If they answer after 4+ rings, they need CallLock. This is the most honest form of prospecting: demonstrate the problem live.

### Method 3: Housecall Pro / Jobber Communities
These platforms have active Facebook groups, forums, and communities. Contractors on these platforms are explicitly NOT on ServiceTitan. They discuss pain points openly. "Missing calls" and "need a receptionist" are common threads.

### Method 4: Home Service Contractor Facebook Groups
Groups like "HVAC Technicians," "Plumbing Contractors," and trade-specific communities. This is where small contractors hang out. Not LinkedIn — Facebook.

### Method 5: Google Business Profile Signals
Contractors with Google Business profiles who:
- Have reviews mentioning "hard to reach" or "didn't answer"
- Have low response ratings
- Are in markets with high LSA competition

## The Positioning Connection

This research validates the Dunford positioning from [[playbooks/positioning-dunford]]:

**Best-fit customer (see [[playbooks/icp-definition]] for full qualification rubric):** HVAC, plumbing, or electrical shop, 2-20 employees, not on ServiceTitan, running paid inbound ads ($1K+/mo), going to voicemail after hours.

**The one-sentence pitch:** "CallLock answers your phone and books jobs after hours — so you stop losing customers to voicemail."

**The LSA-specific pitch:** "You're paying Google $50-$100 for every lead that calls your phone. How many go to voicemail after 5 PM?"

Both work because:
- They start with pain the contractor feels (missed calls, wasted ad spend)
- They imply the ROI without stating it ($297/mo < 4 missed calls)
- No jargon — no "AI," no "platform-agnostic," no "independent contractor"

## Beyond LSA: Every Other Place They Spend Money and Hang Out

### Lead Gen Platforms (they're paying for calls here too)

| Platform | Cost/Lead | Model | Fit for CallLock prospecting |
|----------|-----------|-------|------------------------------|
| **Google LSA** | $35-110 | Pay per lead, exclusive | Best — they're paying most, missing calls hurts ranking |
| **Angi/HomeAdvisor** | $15-100 | Shared leads (2-4 contractors) | Good — shared leads mean speed-to-answer wins the job |
| **Thumbtack** | $15-100+ | Pay when contacted, shared (4-5) | Good — 49% more expensive per booked job than LSA |
| **Yelp** | $300-1,000/mo | Subscription + per-click | Medium — more about reputation than inbound calls |
| **Nextdoor** | Free + $75/deal | Recommendation-based | Great for prospecting — can see who's active locally |

**Key insight:** Contractors on Angi and Thumbtack are getting shared leads (same lead sent to 3-8 competitors). The first to answer wins 35-50% of the time. CallLock's "always answers" value prop is even stronger here than LSA.

**Angi's CAC is ~$2,500** — the highest of any channel. Contractors spending on Angi are bleeding money and would be the most receptive to "stop wasting $2,500 per customer."

### Platform-Specific Prospecting Plays

**Housecall Pro community:** Active Facebook groups and forums. These contractors are explicitly NOT on ServiceTitan. They discuss pain points openly — "missing calls" and "need a receptionist" are common threads. This is where CallLock's best-fit customer self-identifies.

**Jobber community:** Similar to HCP. Skews even smaller (solo to 5-person shops). Price-sensitive. The $297/mo pitch works here.

**Nextdoor:** Contractors with Nextdoor business pages are local, small, and community-oriented. A single recommendation generates 5-15 calls. If they miss those calls, they're wasting the most valuable lead source (neighbor referral). CallLock could partner with Nextdoor-active contractors as early adopters.

## What Could Prove This Wrong

1. Most LSA contractors actually DO answer their calls at 95%+ — the "missed call" problem is overblown for this segment
2. Contractors already use answering services (Ruby, Smith.ai) for this exact problem and are satisfied
3. The non-ServiceTitan market is smaller than assumed — more contractors have migrated to ST than we think
4. Google changes LSA ranking algorithm to de-weight responsiveness
5. Contractors don't trust AI to handle $5,000+ HVAC install leads
