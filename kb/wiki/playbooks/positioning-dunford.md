# CallLock Product Positioning (April Dunford Framework)

Positioning built from the competitive landscape, not from aspirations. Every claim below is grounded in KB research or flagged as unvalidated.

---

## Step 1: Competitive Alternatives

What would CallLock's best-fit customers do if CallLock didn't exist?

### Direct AI alternatives
| Alternative | Who uses it | Why they'd pick it over CallLock |
|-------------|-------------|----------------------------------|
| **ServiceTitan AI Voice Agent** | ServiceTitan customers ($1M+ revenue contractors) | Native integration, zero setup, full CRM data access. Unbeatable on their platform. |
| **Sameday AI** | Larger home services companies (Roto-Rooter, Aptive) | 2M+ calls proven, 92% booking rate, "AI workforce" positioning, enterprise trust |
| **Drillbit** | Residential contractors wanting full lifecycle | CRM + quoting + payments + AI calls — one platform, not point solution |

### Indirect alternatives (the real status quo)
| Alternative | Who uses it | Cost |
|-------------|-------------|------|
| **Answering service** (Ruby, Smith.ai, Nexa) | SMB contractors who want humans | $200-500/mo + per-call fees |
| **Part-time receptionist** | 5-20 person shops | $2,000-3,000/mo |
| **Owner answers the phone** | Solo operators, 1-5 person shops | Free but costs missed jobs |
| **Voicemail / missed calls** | Everyone who can't afford above | Free. Loses 62% of calls (Sameday data) |
| **Do nothing** | Contractors who don't know the problem exists | $0. Leaks ~$80/missed call |

### The honest assessment

For ServiceTitan customers: **ServiceTitan's native AI wins.** Don't compete here on inbound.

For everyone else: The real alternative isn't Sameday or Drillbit. It's **the owner answering when they can and voicemail when they can't.** Most small contractors don't use answering services, don't use AI, and lose calls every day. That's the status quo CallLock has to beat.

## Step 2: Unique Attributes

What does CallLock have (or plan to have) that the alternatives don't?

| Attribute | vs. Status quo (voicemail) | vs. ServiceTitan AI | vs. Sameday | Shipped? |
|-----------|---------------------------|---------------------|-------------|----------|
| AI answers 24/7 | Yes — voicemail doesn't book | They have this | They have this | Yes |
| Works with Housecall Pro, Jobber, pen-and-paper | Irrelevant to status quo | ST only works with ST | Sameday expanding but ST-first | Planned |
| Signal-based outbound prospecting (Scout) | Status quo has nothing like this | ST doesn't do this | Sameday doesn't do this | **Planned, not shipped. Unvalidated.** |
| Price: $297-$797/mo | Cheaper than receptionist, more than voicemail | Cheaper than ST stack | Comparable ($449-$789, unconfirmed) | Yes |
| After-hours specialization | Voicemail is the current after-hours solution | ST AI handles overflow, not after-hours specific | Sameday is 24/7 general | Partial |

### The honest filter

Only TWO attributes are genuinely unique AND verifiable:

1. **Platform-agnostic** — works with contractors NOT on ServiceTitan (Housecall Pro, Jobber, pen-and-paper). This is real because ServiceTitan's AI only works on their platform, and Sameday was historically ServiceTitan-first.

2. **Outbound prospecting (Scout)** — signal-based proactive outreach. Nobody else does this. BUT: it's not shipped, not validated with customers, and there are real reasons competitors avoid it (TCPA, conversion economics, brand risk). See [[competitors/yc-trend-analysis#why-competitors-may-deliberately-avoid-outbound]].

Everything else (24/7 answering, booking, AI quality) is table stakes that every competitor has or will have.

## Step 3: Value

What do those unique attributes ENABLE for the customer?

### Attribute → Value mapping

**Platform-agnostic (shipped):**
- Attribute: Works with Housecall Pro, Jobber, or even pen-and-paper dispatch
- Value: "You don't have to switch to ServiceTitan or pay enterprise prices to get AI call handling. Keep your tools. Add the AI."
- Who cares most: Small contractors (2-15 people) who aren't on ServiceTitan and don't want to be

**Outbound prospecting / Scout (planned, unvalidated):**
- Attribute: AI identifies signals (bad competitor reviews, permit pulls, seasonal triggers) and proactively reaches out to potential customers
- Value: "Stop waiting for the phone to ring. Get new customers who weren't going to call you."
- Who cares most: Growth-oriented contractors who've maxed out their inbound and want to scale
- **CAUTION:** This value prop is hypothetical until validated with real contractors. The wiki's adversarial review flagged that contractors may not want outbound, and there are regulatory risks (TCPA).

**Price (shipped):**
- Attribute: $297/mo starting price
- Value: "Pays for itself with one booked job per month. Costs less than a part-time receptionist."
- Who cares most: Price-sensitive SMBs comparing against $2,000-3,000/mo for a human

## Step 4: Best-Fit Customers

Who cares MOST about the value CallLock delivers?

### Primary: The After-Hours Contractor

**Profile (see [[playbooks/icp-definition]] for full qualification rubric):**
- HVAC, plumbing, or electrical company
- 2-20 employees
- $500K-$3M revenue
- NOT on ServiceTitan (any other FSM or paper is fine)
- Running paid inbound ads (LSA, Angi, Thumbtack — $1K+/mo)
- Goes to voicemail after hours — calls evenings/weekends are lost
- Buyer role is unvalidated: could be owner, office manager, or spouse

**Why they care most:**
- Too small for ServiceTitan's ecosystem (priced out or overbuilt for their needs)
- Too busy to answer every call themselves
- Already spending $1-5K/mo on ads that generate phone calls they miss after hours
- The $297/mo price point is less than 4 missed calls — in their "try it" budget

**What they'd say:** "I know I'm missing calls after 5. I just need something that picks up when I can't."

### Secondary (if Scout validates): The Growth Contractor

**Profile:**
- Same as above, but 10-30 employees, $2M-$10M revenue
- Has a basic CSR/receptionist but wants to grow faster
- Thinks about marketing and lead gen, not just answering calls
- Would pay $500-$800/mo if it provably generates new business

**Why they care most:**
- Inbound is "solved" (they answer most calls) but outbound is zero
- Willing to pay more for revenue generation, not just cost savings
- Signal-based prospecting feels smarter than cold calling

**CAUTION:** This customer segment is entirely hypothetical. Zero conversations validate it.

## Step 5: Market Category

What market does CallLock position in so the value is obvious?

### Option A: Subcategory — pain-first (RECOMMENDED)

**"AI call agent for home service companies"**

- Existing category: "AI call agent" (understood from Sameday, ServiceTitan)
- Modifier: "for home service companies" (specific vertical, avoids "independent contractor" confusion — in the trades that means 1099 worker, not business owner)
- Tagline carries the differentiation: "Stop losing after-hours calls to voicemail"
- Why it works: Category is understood. Vertical is clear. Pain is immediate. Doesn't try to say everything in the category name.

### Option B: Existing category with differentiator

**"AI call agent that works with any field service platform"**

- Category: AI call agent
- Differentiator: platform-agnostic
- Why it could work: Directly addresses the ServiceTitan lock-in problem
- Why it might not: Leads with a technical detail (integration) rather than customer benefit

### Option C: New category (NOT RECOMMENDED)

**"AI revenue engine for home services"**

- Why it's tempting: Captures the outbound/Scout vision, positions above "receptionist"
- Why it's wrong: Requires educating the market on a new concept. CallLock is pre-product with zero budget for category creation. Also, "revenue engine" is exactly the kind of unverifiable marketing spin Dunford warns against.

### Recommendation: Option A

"AI call agent for home service companies" is the category. The tagline does the work: "Stop losing after-hours calls to voicemail." This avoids the "independent contractor" trap (wrong word in the trades), doesn't promise platform-agnostic features that aren't shipped, and leads with pain.

If/when Scout validates, evolve the tagline to: "Stop losing calls. Start finding customers." The outbound becomes a feature within the positioning, not the positioning itself.

## The One-Sentence Test

> "CallLock answers your phone and books jobs after hours — so you stop losing customers to voicemail."

**Pressure test:** If you told this to a 10-person HVAC shop owner at 7 PM after he missed 3 calls on the drive home, would he immediately get it?

- "Answers your phone and books jobs" — that's the problem I have
- "After hours" — that's exactly when I miss calls
- "Stop losing customers to voicemail" — that's the pain I feel every morning checking my phone
- No jargon, no "AI," no "platform-agnostic," no "independent contractor"

The word "AI" is deliberately absent from the one-liner. Contractors don't buy AI — they buy "someone answers my phone." AI is an implementation detail, not a selling point. Mention it in the demo, not the pitch.

**Alternative (if "AI" must be in the sentence for SEO/marketing):**
> "CallLock is the AI call agent that answers your phone after hours — no more lost jobs to voicemail."

---

## What Could Prove This Positioning Wrong

1. "After hours" is too narrow — contractors want 24/7 or nothing, not a part-time solution
2. "AI call agent" becomes too crowded as a category (everyone uses it)
3. Housecall Pro, Jobber, or other FSM platforms ship native AI — every non-ST safe harbor disappears
4. Contractors actually prefer answering services (Ruby, Smith.ai) over AI — human warmth matters more than 24/7 availability
5. The best-fit customer can't justify even $297/mo — the price floor is lower than expected
6. The buyer is the office manager/spouse, not the owner — and the "after-hours voicemail" pitch doesn't resonate with them because they experience the problem differently

## Next Action

**Follow the 3-phase validation plan in [[playbooks/icp-definition]]:**
1. Phone audit: call 50 contractors after hours in Phoenix/Houston/Chicago
2. Discovery interviews: identify the real buyer and validate the pain
3. Commitment test: offer 5 prospects a free 2-week trial

The answers will validate or destroy this positioning. Do this before building anything else.
