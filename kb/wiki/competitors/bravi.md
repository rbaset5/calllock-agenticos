# Bravi

AI operating system for home services installers (shutters, windows, HVAC). YC F25, 2-person team, founder family runs an installation business.

## What They Do

Bravi builds AI voice and chat assistants that replace front office staff for home service installers. Their platform handles inbound communication, lead pre-qualification, callback booking, and automatic quote generation. They are using the founder's family shutter installation business (Volet Dumont) as a live testing ground.

## Why This Matters for CallLock

Bravi is the closest direct competitor in terms of vertical focus and stage. Both target SMB home service companies with AI voice agents. Threat level: **direct competitor**.

Key differences: Bravi leans into quoting automation and a B2B2C manufacturer channel (selling through manufacturers to their installer networks). They are earlier stage (F25, 2 founders) and narrower in initial focus (fenestration/windows). CallLock's planned Scout outbound pipeline (not yet shipped) could be a differentiator if validated -- Bravi appears inbound-only.

## Competitive Intelligence

**Strengths:**
- Strong founder-market fit (family installation business as real testing ground)
- Quote generation feature goes beyond call answering into revenue workflow
- B2B2C manufacturer partnership channel could scale distribution fast
- Prior successful exit (Snipfeed, acquired 2024)

**Weaknesses / Opportunities for CallLock:**
- Tiny team (2 founders) -- limited execution bandwidth
- Initial focus on fenestration is narrow; CallLock can own HVAC and plumbing first
- No outbound capability mentioned -- CallLock's planned Scout could be a wedge (unvalidated)
- No ServiceTitan or major FSM integration mentioned yet
- "AI copilot" positioning is vague compared to "AI sales agent" framing

## Key Takeaways

1. Bravi validates that YC sees the home services AI voice agent category as fundable -- two YC-backed companies (Bravi + Sameday) now target this vertical.
2. Quote generation is a feature CallLock should consider -- automating quotes moves the value prop from "cost saver" to "revenue driver."
3. The B2B2C manufacturer channel is worth watching -- if Bravi gains distribution through manufacturers, they bypass the SMB sales grind.
4. CallLock should move fast on HVAC/plumbing positioning before Bravi expands beyond fenestration.

## Model Translation to CallLock's ICP

Bravi's ICP is one tier up from CallLock's (their floor is $1M revenue, $2K+ AOV, 10–100 employees; CallLock targets 3–15 employee HVAC/plumbing/electrical shops at $500K–$3M). The buyer psychology transfers; the firmographic filters and business-model mechanics do not.

**Copy directly:**
- Done-for-you setup framed as a *feature*, not a service. Owner-operators at any tier refuse to configure workflows.
- Trigger-event list (lost a big lead, hired-and-lost a receptionist, seasonal surge, expanded hours) — identical at every revenue tier.
- Vertical-focus discipline. Bravi owns fenestration before expanding; CallLock should own HVAC/plumbing/electrical before expanding.

**Explicitly reject:**
- Custom enterprise pricing ("book a demo for a quote"). Breaks SMB self-qualification math and signals "not for you" to a 2-truck operator.
- Multi-week CSM-led onboarding. Can't pencil at sub-$10K ACV and 40+ customers/year.
- LinkedIn-primary channel and trade conferences. Owner-operators are not on LinkedIn; they are in their truck.
- Manufacturer + B2B2C "Squad" architecture. That world does not exist at the 2-truck tier.

**Structural inversion:** Bravi optimizes for low-volume/high-ACV (~20 customers ≈ $200K ARR at $10K+ ACV). CallLock must optimize for high-volume/low-ACV (40+ customers at $3.5K–$9.5K ACV). Every GTM choice — pricing, setup, cycle length, channel — flows from that inversion.
