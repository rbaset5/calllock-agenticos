# Sameday AI

AI workforce for the trades — voice AI that answers calls, closes sales, dispatches, and follows up. YC W23. The most direct competitor to CallLock.

## What They Do

Multiple AI agents for home service businesses:
- **AI Receptionist** — answers phones 24/7, books appointments
- **AI CSR** — billing, rescheduling, follow-ups, reminders
- **AI Sales Agent** — sells memberships, negotiates payment plans, overcomes objections, "closes 80% of all sales calls"
- **AI Dispatcher** — prioritizes jobs, manages capacity

This is the "full AI workforce" play — not just a voice bot, but replacing multiple roles.

## Why This Is CallLock's Primary Competitor

Sameday targets the exact same vertical (home services), the exact same problem (missed calls = lost revenue), with the exact same technology (voice AI). Key differences to understand:

### Where Sameday Is Ahead
- **Scale:** 2M+ calls answered, $1.25B revenue facilitated, big-name customers (Roto-Rooter, Aptive, ABC Home Services)
- **Multi-agent approach:** Receptionist + CSR + Sales + Dispatcher = full workforce replacement
- **ServiceTitan depth:** Deep native integration, their anchor platform
- **YC W23** — 3 years head start, established brand in the trades
- **Proven metrics:** 92% booking rate, 96% satisfaction, customers publishing real results

### Where CallLock Can Compete
- **Outbound pipeline:** Sameday is reactive (answers inbound calls). CallLock is building Scout (planned, not yet shipped) — signal-based outbound prospecting. If validated with customers, this is a fundamentally different go-to-market.
- **Multi-platform from day one:** Sameday was ServiceTitan-first. CallLock can target the long tail of contractors on Housecall Pro, Jobber, or even pen-and-paper.
- **Pricing transparency:** Sameday hides pricing behind demos. Transparent pricing builds trust with SMB owners.
- **After-hours focus:** CallLock can own the after-hours window specifically — the highest-value calls that Sameday's data shows have the biggest impact (58 → 208 after-hours bookings).

### Market Data From Sameday's Positioning
- Home service businesses fail to answer **62% of phone calls**
- They pay **4x more per lead** than any other industry
- Missing one sales call costs **$80 on average**
- These stats validate CallLock's entire thesis.

## Pricing Intelligence

Not publicly disclosed. Demo-based enterprise sales process. This suggests higher price points and longer sales cycles. CallLock could undercut with self-serve + transparent pricing for smaller operators.

## Strategic Takeaways

1. **Sameday proves the market exists.** YC-backed, $1.25B revenue facilitated, major customers. The "AI voice agent for trades" category is real and funded.
2. **The potential wedge is outbound.** Sameday is inbound-only. Scout (planned, not shipped) targets signal-based outbound — but this needs customer validation. See [[competitors/yc-trend-analysis]] for why competitors may deliberately avoid outbound.
3. **"AI Sales Agent" positioning wins over "AI Receptionist."** Sameday evolved from receptionist to sales agent framing. CallLock should learn from this — sell revenue generation, not cost savings.
4. **The market problem stats are usable in CallLock's pitch:** 62% of calls missed, $80 per missed call, 4x lead costs. Cite these.
5. **15-minute setup is the bar.** If CallLock takes longer to onboard, that's a problem.

## What Could Prove Us Wrong

- Sameday adds outbound prospecting — with 2M+ calls of training data and existing customer relationships, they could ship this faster and better than CallLock
- Sameday's 92% booking rate is "good enough" — contractors don't need more leads, they need better conversion of existing ones
- ServiceTitan ships native AI call handling — Sameday's integration moat becomes the incumbent's advantage instead
- Sameday's enterprise traction (Roto-Rooter, Aptive) creates a gravity well that pulls SMBs upmarket toward them
