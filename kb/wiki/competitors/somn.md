# Somn

Human-like AI receptionists for healthcare clinics -- handles inbound calls, appointment booking, and outbound patient outreach. YC W24, San Francisco.

## What They Do

Somn builds AI phone agents for healthcare clinics that handle both inbound calls (answering, booking appointments) and outbound calls (proactive patient outreach). They integrate with legacy healthcare infrastructure including fax systems, EHRs, and scheduling software. Their approach is no rip-and-replace -- they work with whatever tech stack the clinic already uses.

## Why This Matters for CallLock

Somn is an **adjacent vertical** competitor, but notable for one critical reason: they do outbound. Most AI voice receptionist companies are inbound-only. Somn proactively calls patients for follow-ups and reminders, which is closer to CallLock's planned Scout outbound pipeline (not yet shipped) than any other competitor in this landscape.

The "no rip-and-replace" positioning is also worth studying. Home service contractors are notoriously resistant to changing their tools. CallLock should emphasize working with existing systems rather than replacing them.

## Competitive Intelligence

**Strengths:**
- Both inbound AND outbound calling -- rare in this space
- Integrates with legacy systems (fax, EHR) -- shows ability to handle messy real-world tech
- Founder-market fit (grew up helping mom run a private practice)
- Healthcare-specific focus enables compliance and workflow depth
- "No rip-and-replace" reduces adoption friction

**Weaknesses / Opportunities for CallLock:**
- Healthcare-only -- no home services overlap
- Small team (only 2 founders listed, no employee count)
- Outbound is patient follow-up (reminders, recalls), not signal-based prospecting like CallLock's planned Scout
- Less market data published compared to Arini or Patientdesk
- Competing with Arini (17 people) and Patientdesk in the same healthcare/dental space

## Key Takeaways

1. Somn validates that outbound AI calling is viable in healthcare -- CallLock's planned Scout pipeline targets a similar capability for home services (signal-based prospecting vs. simple follow-ups). Needs customer validation.
2. "No rip-and-replace" should be a core CallLock message -- contractors will not adopt tools that require changing their workflow.
3. Legacy system integration (fax, old software) is a real moat. Home services has its own legacy systems (paper dispatch, old phone trees) that CallLock should handle gracefully.
4. The healthcare voice AI space is getting crowded (Somn + Arini + Patientdesk). Home services has fewer entrants -- this is a window of opportunity.
