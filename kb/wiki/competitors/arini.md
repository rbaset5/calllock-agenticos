# Arini

AI voice receptionist for dental practices -- answers calls 24/7, schedules appointments, handles insurance inquiries. YC W24, 17-person team, Meta/Threads engineering pedigree.

## What They Do

Arini builds an AI receptionist specifically for dental clinics and Dental Service Organizations (DSOs). It plugs into existing practice management software to book appointments, answer insurance and billing questions, and recover revenue from missed calls. Their founders did door-to-door research shadowing dentists to understand workflows. They target multi-location practices and DSOs for faster revenue scaling.

## Why This Matters for CallLock

Arini is an **adjacent vertical** competitor -- same model (AI voice receptionist for SMBs), different industry (dental vs. home services). Threat level is low for direct competition but high for pattern recognition.

Arini demonstrates what a well-executed vertical AI voice agent looks like: deep PMS integrations, insurance verification, and a $220B market with $7B in front desk staffing spend. CallLock should study their playbook and apply it to home services. The 17-person team for a W24 company also signals strong fundraising -- this category attracts capital.

## Competitive Intelligence

**Strengths:**
- Large, well-resourced team (17 people) for a W24 batch company
- Threads/Meta AI engineering talent (search and AI expertise)
- Deep vertical focus enables dental-specific features (insurance verification, PMS integration)
- Clear market data: 35% of calls missed, 80% of appointments booked by phone, $7B front desk spend
- Multi-location / DSO focus = higher ACV per customer

**Weaknesses / Opportunities for CallLock:**
- Dental-only -- no home services overlap
- No outbound capability mentioned
- Enterprise focus (DSOs) may leave single-location practices underserved
- Insurance verification is dental-specific; home services equivalent (permit/licensing) is less complex
- Dental is getting crowded (Arini + Patientdesk + Somn all targeting healthcare)

## Key Takeaways

1. Arini's market stats are a template for CallLock's pitch: find the equivalent of "35% of calls missed" and "$7B front desk spend" for home services. Sameday's data (62% calls missed, $80 per missed call) fills this gap.
2. The DSO / multi-location play is smart -- CallLock should consider franchise home service brands (Mr. Rooter, One Hour Heating) as high-ACV targets.
3. 17-person team at W24 stage shows investors are funding this category aggressively. CallLock needs a clear differentiation story beyond "same thing for a different vertical."
4. Door-to-door founder research is worth emulating -- deep empathy with the end user builds better products.
