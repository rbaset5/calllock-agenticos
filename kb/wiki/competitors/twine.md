# Twine

AI text message receptionist that auto-texts customers when businesses miss calls. Not voice AI -- SMS-based. YC S23, horizontal SMB focus.

## What They Do

Twine monitors missed calls and immediately texts the customer back via SMS. The AI handles natural language conversations over text -- answering FAQs, booking services, and following up. They target small businesses broadly (horizontal). The core insight is that speed of response matters more than channel -- the first business to respond wins the lead.

## Why This Matters for CallLock

Twine is a **horizontal player** with a fundamentally different modality (text vs. voice). Threat level for direct competition is low, but Twine could be complementary or could erode CallLock's value prop if SMBs decide "just text them back" is good enough.

The key question: do home service customers prefer to call or text? For emergency plumbing or HVAC failures, the answer is almost certainly voice. For routine maintenance scheduling, text might suffice. CallLock should own the high-value emergency/urgent call segment where voice is non-negotiable.

## Competitive Intelligence

**Strengths:**
- Different modality avoids the hard voice AI problems (latency, interruption handling, accent recognition)
- Minimal setup -- integrates with existing VOIP and cell, no complex configuration
- Speed-of-response positioning is backed by real SMB behavior data
- YC S23 -- more mature than many competitors in this analysis
- Founder sold phone systems to SMBs -- deep distribution channel knowledge

**Weaknesses / Opportunities for CallLock:**
- Text-only means they miss the highest-value calls (emergencies, urgent service needs)
- Reactive only (responds to missed calls) -- no proactive outbound prospecting
- Horizontal = no home-services-specific features (dispatch, quoting, ServiceTitan)
- SMS conversion rates are typically lower than live voice for high-ticket services
- No ability to handle complex conversations (insurance claims, multi-step troubleshooting)

## Key Takeaways

1. Twine's "speed of response > quality of response" insight is valid for lead capture. CallLock should emphasize instant answer rates and track time-to-response as a key metric.
2. SMS follow-up for missed calls is a feature CallLock should consider adding as a fallback -- if the AI voice agent can't reach a caller, text them.
3. Voice wins for high-ticket, urgent home services. CallLock should frame this explicitly: "For a $5,000 HVAC install, your customer wants to talk to someone, not text."
4. Twine's horizontal approach means they will never build ServiceTitan integrations or trade-specific workflows -- this is CallLock's moat.
