# Drillbit

AI-powered admin platform for residential contractors covering the full job lifecycle: lead gen through payment collection. YC S24, 10-person team, Austin TX.

## What They Do

Drillbit automates administrative workflows for residential contractors (plumbers, electricians, cleaners, roofers). Their platform includes AI receptionists for phone coordination, LLM-generated quotes, a CRM, project management, and payment processing. The core insight is that half the cost of a home service job is administrative coordination, not labor or materials. They meet contractors where they are -- on the phone -- rather than forcing them into apps.

## Why This Matters for CallLock

Drillbit is a **direct competitor** with a broader scope. While CallLock focuses on the voice agent entry point, Drillbit is building the full back-office replacement (calls + quoting + CRM + payments). This is the "platform vs. point solution" threat.

The risk: if Drillbit wins the CRM/payments layer, their voice agent becomes a free add-on. The opportunity: Drillbit's broader scope means slower execution on any single feature. CallLock can win by being the best voice agent that integrates with existing tools, rather than trying to replace everything.

## Competitive Intelligence

**Strengths:**
- Full lifecycle coverage (lead to payment) -- stickier than call answering alone
- Phone-first UX designed for contractors who hate apps
- Strong founder (Stanford CS/AI, SpaceX, Apple, Rivian, hands-on construction experience)
- Well-resourced (10 people, hiring staff AI engineers at $190-300K)
- $675-700B TAM framing

**Weaknesses / Opportunities for CallLock:**
- Building everything means shipping nothing perfectly -- CRM + payments + quoting + voice is a huge surface area
- "Replace your entire back office" is a harder sell than "never miss a call"
- No outbound prospecting mentioned -- inbound only
- Competing with established CRMs (ServiceTitan, Jobber, Housecall Pro) on their turf
- High churn risk if any single module underperforms -- contractors will compare each piece to best-of-breed

## Key Takeaways

1. Drillbit's "half the cost is admin" stat is powerful -- CallLock can use this to frame the problem without building the full platform.
2. The phone-first approach validates CallLock's core thesis: contractors live on the phone, not in apps.
3. CallLock should avoid the platform trap. Integrate with existing CRMs rather than building one -- let Drillbit fight ServiceTitan while CallLock owns the voice layer.
4. Drillbit's quoting feature is worth monitoring -- if AI-generated quotes become standard, CallLock may need to add this or partner.
