# ServiceTitan AI Voice Agent

ServiceTitan's native AI voice agent — built directly into Contact Center Pro. Not a startup. The incumbent platform adding the feature. This is the highest-threat competitor in the landscape.

## What They Do

A first-party AI voice agent embedded in ServiceTitan's platform. It has immediate access to all company data (job histories, customer records, scheduling, pricing), books jobs in real-time using Adaptive Capacity (matching job type, location, skill sets), and performs side-by-side with human CSRs in the same dashboard.

Setup: 20 minutes. Booking rate: 70-90%. One contractor's AI agent ("Piper") hit a 67% close rate — only 3 points behind human CSRs.

## Why This Is the Real Threat

Every other competitor in this wiki is a startup trying to integrate WITH ServiceTitan. ServiceTitan IS ServiceTitan. The implications are severe:

**1. Zero integration friction.** Sameday, CallLock, Drillbit all need API access, data sync, and configuration to work with ServiceTitan. ServiceTitan's AI already has everything — job history, pricing, scheduling, customer records. No setup. No latency. No data gaps.

**2. Distribution is solved.** ServiceTitan has ~10,000+ contractors on the platform. They don't need to sell AI voice agents to contractors — they just upsell an existing customer base. Every startup in this wiki has to acquire customers one by one.

**3. The integration moat flips.** The trend analysis says "integration depth is the moat." But when the incumbent builds the feature natively, integration depth becomes the incumbent's advantage. "Why would I pay for Sameday when ServiceTitan already does this and it's built in?"

**4. Unified analytics.** AI call performance in the same dashboard as human CSR performance. No context-switching, no second tool. This is what contractors actually want — fewer tools, not more.

## Where ServiceTitan's AI Falls Short

**1. Only for ServiceTitan customers.** The majority of home service companies are NOT on ServiceTitan. They use Housecall Pro, Jobber, pen-and-paper, or nothing. ServiceTitan targets larger operations ($1M+ revenue). The long tail of small contractors is unserved.

**2. Premium tier only.** Part of Contact Center Pro — not available on the base plan. ServiceTitan is already expensive ($300-500+/mo per user). Adding AI is an upsell on top of an upsell. Small contractors can't justify this.

**3. No outbound.** ServiceTitan's AI is inbound-only (answering calls, managing overflow). No signal-based prospecting, no proactive outreach. Same gap as every other inbound player — but this one matters more because if ServiceTitan added outbound, they'd be unstoppable.

**4. 67% close rate vs human 70%.** Good, not great. The gap is small but real. Contractors who care deeply about every call may still prefer humans for high-value jobs.

**5. Platform lock-in.** Only works with ServiceTitan. If a contractor switches to Housecall Pro or Jobber, the AI voice agent goes away. This creates an opportunity for platform-agnostic solutions.

## What This Means for CallLock

**The honest assessment:** If a contractor is on ServiceTitan and happy with it, CallLock has almost no wedge on inbound. ServiceTitan's native AI will be "good enough" and infinitely easier to set up. Competing on inbound against the incumbent platform is a losing game.

**Where CallLock can win:**

1. **The non-ServiceTitan market.** Most contractors aren't on ServiceTitan. Housecall Pro, Jobber, and pen-and-paper operators are underserved. CallLock can be the AI voice agent for the rest of the market.

2. **Outbound (if validated).** ServiceTitan doesn't do outbound. If contractors want proactive prospecting (planned Scout pipeline, not yet shipped), that's a capability ServiceTitan doesn't offer and may not build soon — it's outside their core competency of managing existing customer workflows.

3. **Price.** ServiceTitan + Contact Center Pro + AI Voice Agent = significant monthly cost. CallLock at $297/mo for a contractor on Jobber could be 3-5x cheaper than the ServiceTitan stack.

4. **Multi-platform.** A contractor using Housecall Pro today who might switch to Jobber next year wants an AI agent that works with either. ServiceTitan's AI dies if you leave their platform.

## What Could Prove Us Wrong

- ServiceTitan drops AI voice agent pricing to near-free as a retention/acquisition tool — every contractor gets it for free on any plan
- ServiceTitan adds outbound prospecting natively — they have the data (customer history, job patterns) to do signal-based outreach better than anyone
- Housecall Pro and Jobber build their own AI voice agents — the "non-ServiceTitan market" gets served by other incumbents
- ServiceTitan acquires Sameday or Drillbit — combining startup innovation speed with incumbent distribution

## Key Takeaways

1. **Don't compete on inbound for ServiceTitan customers.** You will lose. Their native integration is unbeatable.
2. **Own the non-ServiceTitan market.** Housecall Pro, Jobber, and pen-and-paper contractors need an AI voice agent too.
3. **Outbound is the only feature play against ServiceTitan.** Everything else they can build natively.
4. **Price is a wedge for smaller contractors** who can't afford ServiceTitan's premium tier.
5. **Watch for Housecall Pro and Jobber adding AI.** If they do, the "non-ServiceTitan" safe harbor shrinks.
