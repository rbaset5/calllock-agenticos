# After-Hours Is the Wedge

## The claim

After-hours is not an edge case for CallLock — it is the primary wedge. Every piece of messaging, pricing, ad copy, and sales script should lead with after-hours coverage before anything else.

## The math

A non-24/7 business with standard trade hours (8a–5p M–F) is staffed roughly **45 hours per week** and unstaffed roughly **123 hours per week**. That means **~73% of the calendar week** is a coverage gap by default. For HVAC, plumbing, electrical, garage door, drain, and water treatment — where emergencies drive the highest-ticket jobs — that 73% is where the money actually lives.

## The blind spot

Owners systematically underestimate this gap because missed after-hours calls are invisible:

- No voicemail left → no record the call happened.
- Customer calls the next shop in the Google results → revenue shows up on a competitor's books, not as a loss on yours.
- Owner checks voicemail at 9pm, sees zero messages, concludes "we're fine."

The gap isn't hidden because owners are dumb. It's hidden because the feedback loop is broken. CallLock's job is to make the invisible visible — and then catch it.

## What this rules OUT

- Stop leading with "we answer calls when you're on a job." That's the secondary wedge (daytime overflow). Use it as a follow-up, never as the opener.
- Stop framing CallLock as a "virtual receptionist" or "answering service." Those categories prime the buyer to think daytime-first.
- Stop benchmarking against Smith.ai or Ruby. They compete on daytime message-taking. Different battlefield.

## What this rules IN

- **Opener (cold call, ads, landing page):** Lead with evenings and weekends. "Most shops are covered 8 to 5 — it's what happens at 9pm on a Saturday that costs you jobs."
- **Demo framing:** Play a real after-hours call clip first. Daytime overflow is the second clip, not the first.
- **ICP filter:** Deprioritize shops that already staff evening phones (they've solved the primary problem). Prioritize shops on paid inbound (LSA, Angi) where every missed after-hours call is wasted ad spend.
- **Pricing anchor:** Frame against lost revenue per weekend, not hours of labor saved. "Three missed Saturday calls at $400 each = $60k/yr leaking."

## Kill criteria

Abandon or revise this thesis if any of the following hold after 30+ validated ICP calls:

1. Owners consistently report after-hours calls convert at <10% of daytime call value.
2. >30% of target ICP already staffs evening phones (spouse, dispatcher, on-call tech) well enough that they do not experience the gap as painful.
3. Validated win/loss data shows CallLock deals close on daytime-overflow framing more often than on after-hours framing.
