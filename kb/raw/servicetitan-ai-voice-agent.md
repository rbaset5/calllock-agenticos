## What It Is

ServiceTitan has built a NATIVE AI Voice Agent directly into their platform. Not a third-party integration — a first-party product built into Contact Center Pro.

"The only AI Voice Agent built exclusively for the trades that books in real-time."

## Key Details

- **Setup time:** 20 minutes to implement, "designed to start accurately and efficiently, booking calls on day one"
- **Booking rates:** 70-90% across real deployments
- **Data access:** AI Voice Agent has immediate access to ALL company data — job histories, customer records, scheduling, pricing
- **Adaptive Capacity:** Books according to job type, location, required skill sets
- **Human handoff:** Recognizes when a live human is needed and transfers seamlessly
- **Call analytics:** AI-generated summaries and transcripts for every call and job
- **Performance tracking:** Performance reviewable side-by-side with live CSRs in the same dashboard

## Case Study: Superior Plumbing

- AI agent named "Piper"
- Piper's close rate: 67% — trailing human CSRs by only 3 percentage points
- "Piper's Got My Back" — contractor testimonial headline
- Voice agents handling more than 20% of total jobs for some businesses
- 60% reduction in missed calls with Contact Center Pro

Part of "Contact Center Pro" — a premium tier of ServiceTitan. Not available on base plan. Pricing not publicly listed but ServiceTitan contracts are typically $300-500+/mo per user.

## Why This Is Different From Sameday/CallLock

ServiceTitan's AI Voice Agent is not a startup competitor. It's the PLATFORM adding the feature natively. Key implications:

1. **Zero integration friction:** The voice agent already has full access to the CRM, scheduling, job history, pricing. No API setup, no data sync, no latency.
2. **Existing customer base:** ServiceTitan has ~10,000+ contractors already paying for the platform. They just upsell the AI module.
3. **Unified analytics:** AI call performance shows up in the same dashboard as human CSR performance. No switching tools.
4. **The integration moat flips:** When the incumbent IS the platform, integrating with them becomes "why not just use their built-in solution?"
